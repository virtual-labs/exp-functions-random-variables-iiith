Instructions
    Quick theory overview:

      To find the cdf \( F_W \) of \( W \), given the cdf \( F_Y \) of \( Y \), we can write:

      \( F_W(w) = P[W \leq w] = P[g(Y) \leq w] \)

      The probability on the right needs to be expressed in terms of \( Y \). If \( g \) is strictly increasing, it admits an inverse function \( g^{-1} \) and we can write:

      \( F_W(w) = P[g(Y) \leq w] = P[Y \leq g^{-1}(w)] = F_Y(g^{-1}(w)) \)

      Procedure:

    • Select the function \( Z = g(X) \) to visualize the PDF of \( Z \).
    • There are two options available: Quadratic and Exponential.
    • We plot the PDF of \( X \) and \( Z \) and visualize the transformation. Also, the function \( g(X) \) is plotted for reference.
    • The plot of the PDF of Z is interactive. You can click on any point in the plot and that will show the corresponding points in the PDF of X.
    • The user is able to visualize how the PDF of Z is obtained from the PDF of X here.
